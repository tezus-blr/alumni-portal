Tezpur University Alumni Association – Bangalore Chapter - Rules and Regulations

Name of the Body:
  • The name of the society - Tezpur University Alumni Association – Bangalore Chapter which shall be known as the Association consequently.
Jurisdiction:
  • The jurisdiction of the Association shall be all over India.
  • Any disagreement / case / suit shall be instituted and shall be subject to the court in Bangalore only.
Membership of the Tezpur University Alumni Association – Bangalore Chapter

All the alumni of Tezpur University subscribe to Bangalore Chapter of the Tezpur University Alumni Association to avail the voting rights.

Block your seats with:
  • For Alumni living in India – Rs. 500 only.
  • For overseas Alumni – US $10.00 only.
Ceasation of Membership:
  • If he/she resigns from his/her membership.
  • If he/she found to be of unstable mind or is convicted by a court for any offence on moral grounds.
  • If he/she is being terminated by the executive committee.

The Executive Committee and Office Bearers of Tezpur University Alumni Association – Bangalore Chapter.

The TU Alumni Association – Bangalore Chapter shall be managed by an Executive Committee framed as follows:
  • One President
  • One Vice President
  • One General Secretary
  • Two Joint Secretary
  • One Treasurer and One Joint Treasurer
  • Twelve Executive Members

The Executive Committee shall have the liberty to choose members from time to time for the well-organized and resourceful functioning of TU Alumni Association – Bangalore Chapter.

Organizational Structure of the Association –

Executive Committee: To manage the affairs of the Association.

The Executive Committee shall consist of –

  • 1. President - 1 (one)
  • 2. Vice President - 1 (one)
  • 3. General Secretary - 1 (one)
  • 4. Joint Secretary - 2 (two)
  • 5. Treasurer - 1 (one)
  • 6. Joint Treasurer - 1 (one)
  • 7. Executive Members - 12 (twelve)
  • The executive committee will generally meet quarterly, but the secretary may call an urgent meeting at any time.
  • Prior notice of 7 days shall usually be given to members for convening a meeting and for urgent meeting 1day prior notice.
  • The quorum for the Executive committee meeting shall be ten. In case there is no quorum it shall be adjourned.
  • In case of any vacancy arise in the Executive committee; the executive committee shall fill this by nomination. A vacancy would be deemed to come up when an executive committee member articulates his/her incapability to discharge the accountability or when a member remains absent for three consecutive meetings without information.
Election of the Executive Committee:
  • All the 19 (nineteen) members of the executive committee shall be elected in AGM through a simple majority of votes.
  • The elected members of the Executive committee shall usually hold office for one year.
  • No elected members of the executive committee shall hold office for more than two successive terms.
  • In case of any absence of application in the election, the members present in the AGM shall nominate to fill in vacancy/vacancies from the members in attendance.
Privileges of the Executive Committee:
  • The Executive shall have all powers with regards to management and promotion of objectives of the Association according to the directive if any of the General body.
  • The Executive committee shall frame bye-laws in accordance with the rules and regulations, and report to the General body, Amendments will be done by a simple majority.
Advisory Committee: To set the strategy and give useful advice to the Executive Committee.

The Advisory Committee shall consist of:

  • Distinguish active members of the association selected by General Body in the AGM or SGBM and have the voting rights.
  • Distinguish active faculty member of Tezpur University selected in AGM/SGBM.
  • Distinguish honorary members from industry selected in AGM/SGBM.
  • Immediate past Office-Bearers nominated by the immediate past executive committee and have the voting rights.
  • The number shall consist from 3 to 7.
  • The members of the Advisory Committee shall normally hold office for two years and can be selected again.
The Office Bearers:

The Office Bearers shall consist of the followings -

  • President
  • Vice President
  • General Secretary
  • Joint Secretary 1
  • Joint Secretary 2
  • Treasurer
  • Joint Treasurer
Election Process of the Office Bearers:
  • The elected members of the Executive Committee shall elect the Office Bearers.
  • The elected members of the Executive Committee shall nominate for the Office Bearers’ position.
  • President, Vice-President, General Secretary, Treasurer, at least one of the joint Secretaries must be stationed in Bangalore.
  • The order of election shall be bottom up, starting with Joint Treasurer to President.
  • The elected members of the Office Bearers shall usually hold office for one year.
  • No elected members of the Office Bearers shall hold office for more than two successive terms.
Powers and Duties of the Office Bearers:

President shall enjoy the power of:

  • The President shall be in charge over the Executive Committee meeting and the General Body meetings. The President shall have the voting power.
  • The President shall handle the committee towards the management and the endorsement to achieve the goals of the union.
  • The President shall sign up all the sub-committee as authorized by the Executive Committee.
Vice President shall enjoy the power of:
  • The Vice President shall perform for the President in absence of the President.
  • The Vice President shall render advice to the Executive Committee with regard to the promotion of the objectives of the Association.
General Secretary shall enjoy the power of:
  • The General Secretary shall be present at all the activities as approved by the Executive Committee.
  • The General Secretary shall be accountable for carrying out all the communication process of the Association.
  • The General Secretary shall arrange and dispense all the publications of the Association.
  • Within a month after AGM, General Secretary shall file the needed information with the Registrar of society.
The Treasurer shall enjoy the power of:
  • The Treasurer shall be responsible for the maintenance of the financial records and accounts of the Association.
  • The Treasurer shall operate the funds of the Association jointly with the Joint Treasurer.
Joint Secretary shall enjoy the power of:
  • The Joint Secretary will assist the General Secretary for the management of the activities of the Association.
  • The Joint Secretary shall act for the Secretary in the absence of the General Secretary.
Joint Treasurer shall enjoy the power of:
  • The Joint Treasurer will assist the Treasurer for the safeguarding of the financial records and accounts of the Association.
  • The Joint Treasurer shall act for the Treasurer in the absence of the Treasurer.
General Body: Includes all the members.
  • To arrange the Annual General meeting at least once a year.
  • An aforementioned notice of clear 21 days for convening an AGM shall generally be given to the members by the General Secretary in discussion with the President.
  • A Special General Body Meeting (SGBM) can only be convened by the President for compelling reasons at a short notice of 5 days to 10 days.
  • The quorum of a General Body Meeting shall be thirty (30) members present in person. If the meeting is adjourned for want of quorum, the adjourned meeting shall be convened by sending notice again to the members to manage the same business and members shall form the quorum.
Duties and responsibilities of the General body:
  • To adopt and think about the Annual Report and Audited Accounts of the Association.
  • To grant the budget for the subsequent year.
  • To honour the Jubilee Alumni and Distinguished Alumni.
  • To elect the members of the Executive Committee.
  • To handle the business in the General Body Meeting.
Finance and Accounts Committee:
  • The Association shall raise funds for pursuing the objectives of the Association through.
  • Subscription from the members the rates of subscription shall be governed by the By-Laws of the Association.
  • Money donated by the Members.
  • Any other source approved by the Executive Committee.
Duties and responsibilities of the Finance and Accounts Committee:
  • The Accounts of the Association shall be maintained in any scheduled bank in Bangalore in to which all subscription donations and other income shall be credited.
  • The financial year of the Association shall be from April 1st to March 31.
  • The funds of the Association may be invested in Government securities.
  • Withdrawal from the invested funds shall be made by the Executive Committee.
  • The money raised through membership shall not be spent for any purpose. However, interest accruing thereon can be utilized through the approval of the Executive Committee.
  • Regular audit of Accounts.
Rules and Regulations Amendment of Tezpur University Alumni Association – Bangalore Chapter:
  • Amendment to the rules and regulations shall be carried out only by a General Body meeting when a minimum of three fifths of the Majority votes of the members present in the meeting.
  • The proposed amendment to the rules and regulations shall be circulated to all the members as an agenda item for the General Body Meeting.
Suit and Proceeding By and Against the Association
  • The secretary or any other office bearer authorized by the Executive Committee may sue on behalf of the Association. When the Association is being sued, the secretary will represent the Association.
  • No suit or proceeding shall fail by reason of any vacancy or change in the holder of the office of the secretary or any office bearer authorized on its behalf.
  • Every decree or order against the Association in any suit or proceeding shall be executable against the property of the Association and not against the property of the secretary.
